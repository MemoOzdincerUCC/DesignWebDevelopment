The Education Crisis; A Systematic Cyle of Poverty

School funding inequality has been a prevalent factor in the poverty cycle across The US and Canada. Across all states and provinces, school boards allocate drastically different amounts of money to districts, solely based on the property tax of that area. Although this might have been a viable strategy in the incipient stages of America, today, it is the prevailing problem in the education industry. Schools in neighborhoods that have valuable properties are given adequate funding and resources, whereas, ones in impoverished communities are left to struggle.
